# Work Distribution: Admin, Employee, and Customer

This document describes the Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) and specific responsibilities of each user type in the Modern Banking System.

## 1. Super Admin & Admin Roles
Super Admin owns ultimate control and cannot be deleted or demoted. Admin oversees daily operations.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Managing users (viewing and updating roles).
  - Monitoring system statistics (total users, total bank balance, total transactions).
  - Approving or rejecting loan applications.
  - Viewing the transaction history of all accounts.
  - Super Admin can create Admins; Admin cannot create Super Admins.
  - Admin deletion is blocked for both Admin and Super Admin accounts.

## 2. Employee Role (Operational Access)
Employees act as bank tellers and customer support.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Onboarding new customers (creating customer accounts and initial bank accounts).
  - Viewing the customer list to assist with account-related issues.
  - Managing account statuses (activating or blocking accounts).
  - **Constraint**: Employees cannot apply for loans or perform transfers on behalf of customers for security reasons.
  - All accounts they create are attributed to them for audit (“Created By”).

## 3. Customer Role (Personal Access)
Customers can only access their own data and perform limited financial activities.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Viewing their own account balances and account types.
  - Transferring funds to other accounts (up to Rs. 50,000 limit).
  - Applying for loans and viewing the status of their loan applications.
  - Viewing their personal transaction history.
  - Managing their profile (updating name, email, phone, etc.).
  - Customer records include “Created By” for audit.
